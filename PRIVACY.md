# Privacy Policy for Hearth

_Last updated: 2026-07-04_

Hearth is a habit-tracking app built to work entirely on your device. This policy explains, plainly, what that means.

## What Hearth collects

Nothing. Hearth does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data, usage data, or analytics. It has no servers, no accounts, and no network connections of any kind.

## Where your data lives

Everything you enter into Hearth — your name, habits, completion history, and notes — is stored locally on your device in a private database. Your PIN, if you set one, is hashed and stored in your device's secure credential storage (Android Keystore). None of this ever leaves your device.

## Permissions Hearth requests

- **Notifications** — used only to ask the operating system for permission to show local reminder notifications you configure yourself. No notification content is sent anywhere.
- **Biometric unlock** (fingerprint/face) — used only to unlock the app locally, via your device's built-in biometric system. Hearth never has access to your biometric data itself; the OS handles verification and only tells Hearth "yes" or "no."

## Sharing your data

Hearth includes an "Export data as JSON" feature in Settings. This is entirely user-initiated: it creates a file on your device and hands it to your device's native share sheet, so you can choose to save or send it wherever you like. Hearth itself does not upload or transmit this file anywhere.

## Deleting your data

Settings includes a "Reset all data" option that permanently deletes everything Hearth has stored on your device. Uninstalling the app has the same effect.

## Children's privacy

Hearth does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children, because it does not collect information at all.

## Changes to this policy

If Hearth's data practices ever change, this page will be updated and the "Last updated" date above will reflect that.
